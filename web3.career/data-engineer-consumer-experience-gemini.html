The Role: Data Engineer

Responsibilities:

  • Conceptualize and own the data architecture for large-scale projects, while evaluating design and operational cost-benefit tradeoffs within systems.
  • Collaborate with engineers, product managers, and data scientists to understand data needs, representing key data insights visually in a meaningful way.
  • Build and maintain data dictionaries and process documentation.
  • Develop new systems and tools to enable the teams to consume and understand data more intuitively.
  • Optimize pipelines, dashboards, frameworks, and systems to facilitate easier development of data artifacts.
  • Influence product and cross-functional teams to identify data opportunities to drive impact.
  • Solve our most challenging data integration problems, utilizing optimal ETL patterns, frameworks, query techniques, sourcing from structured and unstructured data sources.

Minimum Qualifications:

  • 5+ years experience working in a data or analytics engineering role.
  • Experience in designing, building, and administering modern data pipelines within data warehouses.
  • Advanced SQL skills, with experience transforming raw data into clean models, optimizing SQL performance, and troubleshooting & improving others' code.
  • Strong understanding of data warehousing concepts (e.g. ELT, schema management, materialization) and data modeling concepts (e.g. Star Schema).
  • Experience with Snowflake, BigQuery, or Redshift.
  • Experience with one or more MPP system (Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift, Databricks, etc).
  • Experience with business intelligence solutions (Looker, Tableau, Periscope, Mode).
  • Experience analyzing data to identify gaps and inconsistencies.
  • Experience in working collaboratively across different teams and departments.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Knowledge and practical application of Python.
  • Experience working autonomously in with distributed teams.
  • Experience influencing product decisions with data.
  • Experience working with AWS technology stack.
